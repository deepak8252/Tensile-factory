TYPES OF RETRACTABLE ROOFS

There are various varieties of retractable roofs, each with its own pros and limitations. The most popular form of retractable roof is the single-ply membrane roof. These roofs are built from a single sheet of PVC or TPO (thermoplastic olefin) and are generally less costly than other forms of retractable roofs. However, they are also less sturdy and can be damaged by severe winds or heavy snowfall.

Another form of retractable roof is the double-membrane roof. These roofs are manufactured from two layers of PVC or TPO and are more durable than single-ply membrane roofs. They are also more costly but can resist greater winds and heavier snowfall. The last form of retractable roof is the metal panel roof. These roofs are manufactured from panels of aluminum or steel and are the most durable form of retractable roof. They are also the most costly yet can resist the highest winds and heaviest snowfall.
